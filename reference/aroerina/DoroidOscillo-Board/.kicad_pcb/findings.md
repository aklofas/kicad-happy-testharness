### Incorrect
- The source value field is 'ABLS-12.000MHZ-B4-T', which contains '12.000MHZ'. The crystal_circuits entry has frequency: null. The analyzer should parse the frequency from the value string. 12 MHz is clearly encoded.
  (signal_analysis)
- The schematic has U10 (USBLC6-2SC6) which is a USB ESD protection device explicitly named for USB clamping. It is connected to USB_D+ and USB_D- nets. The design_observations report has_esd_protection: false for both USB_D- and USB_D+, and the protection_devices list is empty. The USBLC6-2SC6 lib_id is 'USBLC6-2SC6' which should trigger USB ESD recognition.
  (signal_analysis)
- U3 is an AMS1117-3.3 LDO (instantiated via AP1117D33 symbol). Its input connects to the VBUS rail (5V from USB), and its output drives ALT_3.3VD. The analyzer reports topology='unknown', input_rail=null, output_rail=null. The output rail ALT_3.3VD is a named power symbol used extensively throughout the schematic. The input VBUS is also a named global label.
  (signal_analysis)
- ALT_VAA- is a negative supply rail generated by the LM2664 charge pump (U6) to power the op-amps (U5) for analog signal processing. It appears at the supply pins of U7 (74HC4052PW mux) and U5 (OPA4354). It is classified as 'signal' in net_classification rather than a power domain net. Similarly ALT_VAA+ is classified as 'signal' rather than 'power'. ALT_3.3VD is also classified as 'signal'.
  (signal_analysis)
- USB_VBUS is a local label connected to the USB VBUS power line near U10 (USBLC6-2SC6) and R29/R31 (a pull-up voltage divider). It is a power rail net, not a high-speed signal net. The net_classification assigns it 'high_speed' because it contains 'USB', but this is a power net, not a differential data line.
  (signal_analysis)
- The differential_pairs list includes ALT_VAA+ / ALT_VAA- as a differential pair. These are not a differential signal pair — they are independent positive and negative supply rails (±analog supply) for the op-amp and mux circuits, generated by the LM2664 charge pump. They happen to share the 'ALT_VAA' prefix, but this is a naming coincidence; they serve completely different roles (power rails, not complementary signals).
  (signal_analysis)

### Missed
- In the source, C36 (18p, at P 14650 7950) and C38 (18p, at P 15150 7950) are connected to the two pins of Y1 (at P 14900 7650). Both go to GND-RESCUE via #PWR034. The crystal_circuits entry reports load_caps: [] instead of [C36, C38]. The load capacitance is 18pF per pin, consistent with the 12 MHz crystal spec.
  (signal_analysis)
- The schematic shows two precision attenuator chains from J1 (BNC input). The low-range channel uses R2 (1100k), R3 (910k), CT1 (3p-10p trimmer), and C5 (10p) to form a frequency-compensated 40% divider. The high-range channel uses R4 (1.96M), R5 (36k), CT2 (1.4p-3p trimmer), and C6 (220p) for a 1.8% attenuator. These are classic probe-style compensation dividers. voltage_dividers: [] in the output.
  (signal_analysis)
- Multiple RC filter pairs surround U7 (74HC4052PW mux) and the op-amp inputs. For example: R13 (3.3k) + C28 (15p) on the channel 1 path, R14 (22k) + C29 (3.3p), R16 (13.3k) + C32 (4.7p), R15 (5.6k) + C31 (10p). These form anti-aliasing filters for the ADC front end. rc_filters: [] in the output.
  (signal_analysis)
- U5 units 1-4 are op-amp signal conditioning stages. All 5 units (including the power unit) are reported as configuration 'unknown'. The neighbor_components lists are empty for all subcircuit entries. For a design this complex (oscilloscope front-end), the analyzer is providing no useful topology information about the op-amp stages despite extensive passive component context.
  (signal_analysis)
- The schematic has a clear I2C bus: nets I2C_SDA and I2C_SCL connect U13 (AT24C02C-XHM-T, I2C EEPROM) and U14 (MCP4726A0T-E/CH, I2C DAC) to U1 (LPC4370, master). Pull-up resistors R20 (4.7k) and R22 (4.7k) connect SDA/SCL to ALT_3.3VD. Test points TP1 (SCL) and TP2 (SDA) are on the bus. bus_analysis.i2c: [] in the output, and I2C_SDA/I2C_SCL nets have empty pins lists.
  (signal_analysis)
- Nets SPIFI_CS, SPIFI_SCK, SPIFI_MOSI, SPIFI_MISO, SPIFI_SIO2, SPIFI_SIO3 connect U1 (LPC4370) to U8 (W25Q80BVSSIG SPI flash). This is clearly a SPIFI (SPI Flash Interface) bus. bus_analysis.spi: [] in the output.
  (signal_analysis)
- U6 (LM2664) is a switched-capacitor voltage inverter (charge pump) that takes ALT_VAA+ and generates ALT_VAA-. It is accompanied by C4, C18, C25, C26, C27, L2, L4 on the supply rails. This is the negative rail generator for the analog front-end. The power_regulators list contains only U3; U6 is missing entirely.
  (signal_analysis)
- U2 and U4 are CPC1017N photoMOSFET optocouplers used as DC/AC coupling switches on the oscilloscope input signal path. They are driven by signals DC_SW and OP_SW. The isolation_barriers list is empty. These are classified as 'ic' but not identified as switching/isolation components.
  (signal_analysis)
- Every named net in the nets section has pins: []. For example, I2C_SDA has point_count: 7 but pins: []. Only nets with explicit pins defined in the component symbol (like GND, ALT_VAA-, ALT_VAA+ via L2/L4 pins, and D1/D2 pins) have populated pin lists. This means no pin-to-net mapping is available for any IC or most passives, making voltage divider detection, RC filter detection, and bus protocol detection impossible. Only the 2 inductors (L2, L4) and D1, D2, P1, Y1 have populated pins because they have explicit pin definitions in the legacy library.
  (signal_analysis)
